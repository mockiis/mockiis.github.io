My Works

Moon Cheese

Moon Cheese is a simple arcade game, it was meant to be more of a test if i could create a game that could be put on XBLIG and Windows Phone, and see what it takes to pass all verifications and test on the way to those markets.
Worked great as a learning tool while it ended a bit on light side for the game part.

Find out more

Light Zone

Light Zone is a Local Multiplayer game for up to 8 players, where you control a spaceship out of control and must hunt down the other players.
A quite fun game that works best with the higher player count, and have since it’s first iteration received quite a lot of changes, and it’s being reworked and fitted into Sons of Eye for a more compelling package.

Being reworked

Shipquest / Mazequest

Original intended as a procedurally generated quest based game, as part of my final thesis but some complications arose and we decided to it’s result was no good and started all over
So we made Mazequest in its wake and are now in the final parts of our thesis and once it’s done should be uploaded together with Shipquest.

Try it here

Sons of Eye

Local Multiplayer game set in space where you have to manipulate gravity to your advantage to get on top.
While working on the single-player portion of the game i found it to be not as fun as i hoped, and then decide to scrap that part of it and instead i’m working on making it as a collection of local-multiplayer games together with Light Zone and other prototypes and ideas, kinda like a olimpics game.

Try it here

Nukeplomacy *TEMP*

An online multiplayer only game of diplomacy and blowing other people up.
Based on nuclear war ideas like Wargames and Defcon with a mixture of some boardgames mechanics.
Would you resort to conflict and use your nukes or try to be diplomatic and stay out of trouble?
The game is coming along just fine and should be done approximately somewhere before summer.

Work in progress

Who is Mocke

More formally know as Joaquin, Mocke is a nice middle ground for some people having trouble on how to pronounce my name.
Currently I'm working on finishing my final thesis, because of some complications last year I had to do it this year, and should be done soon before summer.

I'm foremost a game-programmer, but I'm quite versatile to adapt to any coding language or type of coding whenever is web or software, while also I'm skilled enough in digital arts so that I can implement my own art for the most part speeding up the process in the beginning phases of a new project.

My most used languages at the moment are C# and C++, but i do have have some experience with Java, Javascript, Python, HTML5, PHP, to name a few.

Well that'll a short introduction, if you want to know more drop me a message.
